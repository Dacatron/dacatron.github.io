OUR STORY

This journey started back when we first purchased our 6th Gen Toyota 4Runner back in August of 2025. Before taking delivery, I had done thorough research on the vehicle and discovered that our specific trim came equipped with a JBL Flex, a portable Bluetooth speaker that can be removed from the center dashboard. At first, it seemed like a great feature and gimick but I quickly realized that this gimick has it's drawbacks.

1. The location of this speaker was directly in view of any passer-bys, making it a prime target for theft.

2. Living on the East Coast, the extreme heat in the summer would absolutely cook the JBL Flex (and potentially cause a fire) while the extreme cold would freeze the speaker, completely destorying the battery health and potentially damaging the electronics in the speaker itself, a risk that I am not going to take.

Immediately upon delivery, I immediately removed the JBL Flex and have been using it in my home -away from the harsh temperatures- ever since. Unfortunately, this meant that everytime I get in my brand new vehicle, I would see a large cavity in the center of the dashboard where the Flex once sat. This was something that I didn't want to see, especially when I paid a pretty penny for this vehicle. After dealing with the crater in the dash for a week, I finally decided to design a flat cover that would simply lock into the empty dock, ultimately covering the crater in the dash and protecting the dock itself from the elements once and for all. It took me several weeks and 3 different iterations before I perfected the angles and was satisfied. The final design is now known as the Dacatron Stealth.
